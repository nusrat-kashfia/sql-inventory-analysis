# Business Recommendations

## Project Objective

The objective of this analysis was to examine inventory, sales, warehouse, and procurement data to identify opportunities for improving inventory management, purchasing decisions, and operational efficiency.

---

# Key Findings

## 1. Inventory Management

Several products were found to have inventory levels below their defined reorder points.

### Recommendation

- Replenish these products as a priority to reduce the risk of stock-outs.
- Review reorder points periodically based on recent sales trends.

---

## 2. Inventory Value

A small number of products account for a significant portion of the total inventory value.

### Recommendation

- Monitor these high-value products closely.
- Maintain appropriate stock levels while avoiding excess inventory.

---

## 3. Slow-Moving Products

Some products have high inventory levels but relatively low sales volume.

### Recommendation

- Reduce future purchasing quantities.
- Consider promotional campaigns to reduce excess inventory.
- Review whether these products should remain in the product portfolio.

---

## 4. Warehouse Performance

Inventory levels vary considerably across warehouses.

### Recommendation

- Redistribute inventory between warehouses when appropriate.
- Balance stock levels based on regional demand.

---

## 5. Sales Performance

Certain product categories consistently generate higher revenue than others.

### Recommendation

- Prioritize inventory planning for high-performing categories.
- Allocate purchasing budgets based on demand patterns.

---

## 6. Procurement Performance

Supplier delivery performance varies across purchase orders.

### Recommendation

- Monitor supplier lead times regularly.
- Review suppliers with frequent delivery delays.
- Develop supplier performance scorecards using delivery KPIs.

---

# Overall Business Recommendations

Based on the SQL analysis, the following actions are recommended:

1. Prioritize replenishment for products below their reorder point.
2. Reduce purchasing for slow-moving inventory.
3. Monitor high-value inventory to reduce holding costs.
4. Improve supplier performance monitoring using delivery metrics.
5. Review warehouse inventory distribution regularly.
6. Use sales trends to improve purchasing decisions.
7. Continuously monitor inventory KPIs to support data-driven decision making.

---

# Conclusion

This analysis demonstrates how SQL can be used to transform raw operational data into meaningful business insights.

The queries developed in this project can support inventory planning, procurement decisions, warehouse management, and overall supply chain performance.
